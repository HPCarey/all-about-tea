Why are you here?

If you came here for taste

Maybe your bored of your daily cup of muddy water (no offense), or perhaps you once tasted a truly masterful cup of high mountain oolong and you can't get it off your mind. The world of tea has a bountiful taste experience waiting for you, but it's hard to know where to start. We can guide you on your journey into species, varieties and sub-varieties. For starters, most people know of at least 2 types of tea, black and green. And while a whole host of other plant species often gets thrown into the mix ( mint, camomile, nettle, ginger etc.) the tea plant is a specific plant, and is the only "real" producer of tea leaves.

Tea is any leaf produced by the plant "Camellia Sinensis". Within this species there are several varieties but the two that are mostly used to make tea are the Sinensis and Assamica varieties. As you learn more about tea you'll begin to see the significance of these two species and the types of tea they produce, but for now, taking note of which one you are drinking when you try a new tea is a good start!

If you came here for health

A lot of people's gateway into the world of tea is finding a healthy alternative to coffee, which we are completely on board with. The need to start the day with a quick fix is real, even in the chilled out world of tea! Many people think that tea is a healthier option because it has less caffeine than coffee and this might feel like more of a self-punishment or sacrifice for the sake of one's health. Well lucky for you we can de-myth this idea. In fact, as well as often having up to 3 times as much caffeine as a cup of coffee, a serving of high quality tea has far more benefits than this. For instance, the reason you don't feel as wired after a cup of tea is that, along with caffeine, the tea plant is also rich in a compound called L-theanine, which is known to lower blood pressure and give the drinker a calm and clear-minded feeling.

The other reassurance we'd like to make to those of you who are interested in tea for health is that healthy tea doesn't have to taste bad! If you've ever had a supermarket tea bag of green tea, well let's just say we can picture your initial expression after tasting it for the first time! But please don't give up on green tea as a result of this unfortunate experience. There are numerous varieties and production methods of green tea, and while not all will be to your taste, there are some truly delicious ones out there! Many of our community members are farmers and tea sellers who offer small tasting packs of different varieties of tea. So invest in some high quality tea leaves and taste the difference!

If you came here for a mindful tea practice

Perhaps a small, but dedicated subsection of the tea -lovers community, are those that don't just see tea as a beverage, but as a practice, a philosophy and a way of life. As someone who was initially drawn to the cultural expressions and social role of tea in Taiwanese society, I know the depth of this devotion. To many who don't know this side to tea culture, it may seem odd and a bit intense to be so "into" tea. But anyone who understands the value of mindfulness practices and non-material philosophies of life, will eventually see the profound impacts having a tea practice can offer.

Many meditation techniques try to cultivate a state of focused-attention in order to improve brain-function, while other techniques focus more on transcendental experiences and cultivating inner calm. Well, tea ceremony can offer both of these within the same session! Similarly, if you have trouble with traditional methods of meditation, perhaps due to trauma, anxiety or neurobehavioural reasons like adhd, tea ceremony can offer an effective alternative which is not so mind-focused.

While it takes some initial skill-learning and practice, once you have the techniques, you can more or less practice on your own every day as part of your morning ritual (which conveniently includes your caffeine hit!), or as part of a group to share the experience.

types of tea

Now to the good stuff, other than our good-old, breakfast, black tea, what are the different tea varieties, how are they produced and what do they taste like? Well, there are 6 main types of tea and they are as follows: White, black, green, yellow, oolong and post-fermented tea sometimes known as "dark tea". Within these 6 types of tea there are numerous varieties. In terms of taste, you can bet that there is as much variety in tea as there are flavours of Italian ice-cream and potentially more! health

So, what are the health benefits of drinking tea? Well, there are numerous health benefits that are associated with L-theanine alone, but most high quality teas contain many other valuable compounds, such as catechins, anitoxidants and polyphenols. According to research, regularly drinking tea containing these compounds can boost metabolism, regulate blood-sugar, reduce blood-pressure, improve skin health, help anti-aging as well as mitigate the effects of sun damage to name a few. And more exciting than all that, is the research coming out of X University that suggests not only does tea help prevent cancer, but Matcha tea in particular may actually be the only known ingestible substance that can kill certain cancer cells! Speaking of Matcha, most people interested in tea for health reasons probably associate health benefits with green tea varieties and Matcha. It's true that Matcha probably has the lion's share of health benefits due to the fact that you are actually consuming the leaf, not to mention the rigorous production methods it goes through to produce higher levels of chlorophyll, giving it that vibrant green colour. However, we are here to assure you that, if like many people, green tea is not to your taste, all tea varieties have similar health benefits and perhaps numerous more as there has been a lack of research in the health benefits of other varieties thanks to green tea stealing the show somewhat!

tea ceremony

Oftentimes, traditional meditation can feel like a means to an end, intentionally done to get a specific result. It takes effort, and if you have any little demons running around in your mind, it can be uncomfortable. In tea ceremony, there is only making, serving and drinking tea. Yet somehow, without realising, or even trying, one has achieved focused-attention, calmness and inner-peace. And if the atmosphere is right, one can even experience a profound sense of being and a gratitude for life. And all the while, there is a delicious cup of tea waiting for you at the end For those who are interested there is much to learn in tea ceremony. There are many different practices, some more formal, some more meditative, some focused on the spiritual principles of Zen Buddhism or Taoism, and some even verging on the informal and social styles. There are regional differences and within those, there are different schools with different techniques and styles. There are also various tea implements and teaware that sometimes require quite technical skills to master.
